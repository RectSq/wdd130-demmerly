History

  • 1950s-1960s: The First Experiments
    • Early computers in universities run some of the first digital games.
    • "Tennis for Two" (1958) and "Spacewar!" (1962) show how simple interactive systems can entertain.
    • Games are mostly tech demos â€” no commercial industry yet.
  • 1970s: Arcades & the Birth of the Industry
    • Arcade machines become mainstream.
    • Pong (1972) kicks off the commercial video game industry.
    • Designers focus on simple, addictive gameplay loops.
  • 1980s: The Golden Age
    • Home consoles like the NES bring gaming into households.
    • Super Mario Bros. (1985) defines platformer design.
    • The Legend of Zelda (1986) introduces open-world exploration.
    • Game design becomes more structured with levels, mechanics, and storytelling.
  • 1990s: 3D Graphics & Bigger Worlds
    • Technology jumps from 2D to 3D.
    • DOOM (1993) popularizes first-person shooters and modding.
    • Final Fantasy VII (1997) shows emotional, cinematic storytelling in games.
    • Game studios grow, and genres expand.
  • 2000s: Online Play & Player Communities
    • MMOs and online shooters reshape design around social interaction.
    • World of Warcraft (2004) and Halo 3 (2007) influence multiplayer design.
    • Improved hardware allows bigger worlds and more realistic physics.
  • 2010s: Indie Revolution
    • Small teams and solo developers create hit games.
    • Minecraft (2011) revolutionizes sandbox creativity.
    • Stardew Valley (2016) shows the power of cozy, handcrafted design.
  • 2020s-Now: Art, Narrative, & Innovation
    • Games blend emotional storytelling, art, and accessibility.
    • Spiritfarer (2020) inspires a new wave of gentle, soulful experiences.
    • VR, AR, and AI tools expand what developers can create.
    • Players value aesthetic, community-driven, and small-team projects more than ever.
